Why is it necessary to detect disease in plant ?

Crop Loss Prevention: Each year, plant viruses and fungal attacks lead to crop losses of up to 30%. By identifying diseases early, farmers can take timely action to prevent further spread and minimize damage.

Timely Intervention: When a disease becomes visible, it is often too late to counteract the damage. Early detection allows farmers to intervene promptly, whether through targeted treatments, adjusting irrigation, or other management practices.

Cost-Effective Measures: Traditional laboratory tests for disease detection are expensive and time-consuming. Researchers are now developing low-cost, on-site quick tests that provide farmers with immediate results. These tests help save resources and enable timely decision-making.

Food Security: Plant diseases can devastate entire crops, affecting food availability and prices. Detecting diseases early helps maintain food security by ensuring stable crop yields.

Environmental Impact: Effective disease management reduces the need for excessive pesticide use, minimizing environmental pollution and promoting sustainable agriculture.
